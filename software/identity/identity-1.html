When you log in to a website, your identity must be verified and managed.

Here's how different solutions work:

  • Session: The server stores your identity information and provides your browser with a session ID cookie. This enables the server to monitor your login status, though cookies have limitations when used across multiple devices.
  • Token: Your identity is securely encoded within a token delivered to your browser. On subsequent requests, your browser automatically sends this token for authentication.
  • JWT: JSON Web Tokens offer a standardized approach to identity tokens through robust digital signatures.
  • SSO: Single Sign-On uses a centralized authentication service that allows one set of login credentials to grant access across multiple websites.
