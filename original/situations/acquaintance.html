знако́мство (Acquaintance)

Meeting people

"Hello" is a greeting that can be used in any moment, day or night. In Russia, they have two ways of saying hello. The only difference is that one form mostly used in formal situations while the other one is suitable for more informal contexts.

Здравствуйте (zdrást-vuî-tye) formal

Hello

This "hello" is used with strangers, old people (even if we know them) or with clients. It is used in formal contexts.

Приве́т (pree-vyét) informal

Hello / Hi

This "hello" is used with friends or children. Your common sense should tell you in which other situations you can use it.

A word like "hello" is very useful. But it's good to know what greetings can be used during the day, afternoon, evening,...

До́брое у́тро (dó-bra-ye ú-tra) formal

Good morning

До́брый день (dó-bryj dyen) formal

Good day / good afternoon

This literally means "good day" and can be used during most part of the day, for example, during the usual office hours of 9am to 5pm. As a rule of thumb, you can use it from 10am until the moment when you would say "good evening"

До́брый ве́чер (dó-bryj vyé-chyer) formal

Good evening

You might then introduce yourself

Меня́ зову́т... (mye-nyá za-vút...)

My name is...

Как вас зову́т? (kak vas za-vút?) formal

What's your name?

Как тебя́ зову́т? (kak tye-byá za-vút?) informal

What's your name?

In Russian (like in many other languages) there are two ways of saying "you/your": a formal and an informal one. Use the informal way mostly with friends and children. In any other case (strangers, clients, old people,...) use the formal one. And remember that you can always make use of your common sense to understand which way you should choose.

О́чень прия́тно (ó-chyen pri-yát-na)

Pleased to meet you

This is an absolutely common question to ask when meeting someone. Maybe it's not the most original one, but it's easy to remember, the answer is never something personal and we can use it to start a nice conversation:

Отку́да вы? (at-kú-da vy?) formal

Where are you from?

Отку́да ты? (at-kú-da ty?) informal

Where are you from?

Я из... (ya iz...)

I am from...

Examples:

  • Я из Англии (Ya iz Anglii), I am from England
  • Я из России (Ya iz Russii), I am from Russia

It's always nice to say "how are you?", even when we don't have much to say.

Как дела́? (kak dye-lá?)

How are you?

Спаси́бо, хорошо́. А у вас? (spa-sí-ba, ha-ra-shó. a u vas?) formal

Fine, thank you. And you?

Спаси́бо, хорошо́. А у тебя́? (spa-sí-ba, ha-ra-shó. a u tye-byá?) informal

Fine, thank you. And you?

Pay attention to this: In Russian they don't say "fine, thank you" but "thank you, fine". It's important for you to know this, so you can understand that спасибо means “thank you” and хорошо means “fine” (or "good").

Норма́льно (nahr-mahl'-nah)

Normal or Okay

Здо́рово! (sto-ra-va)

Great!

Пло́хо (plo-ha)

Bad
